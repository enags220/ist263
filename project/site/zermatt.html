Zermatt

In 2019, my senior year of high school, my family took a trip to Zermatt, Switzerland to ski. Zermatt is the one place my dad had always wanted to visit and for good reason. Zermatt is located in the southern region of Switzerland only accessible by train, helicopter, or car to the edge of the town. Zermatt is not only famous for its world renowned skiing, but it is also home of the Matterhorn; commonly recognizable for being the mountain on the Toblerone chocolate bar. The town of Zermatt is completely isolated from the outside world. The first thing you notice is that there are no gas powered cars. The only cars you will see are small, battery powered taxis. This is to reduce air pollution that would obscure the view of the Matterhorn.

My family arrived in Zermatt by way of a long taxi ride in February of 2019. Hoping for plentiful snow, we were unfortunately met with warm conditions and no new snowfall for weeks. While I didn’t get to experience the amazing skiing Zermatt has to offer, my family and I appreciated the beautiful terrain of the mountains and the vast culture in the town. While the skiing was not the best for us, the memories and beauty solidify Zermatt as one of the most extraordinary ski resorts I’ve ever been to.

While in Switzerland, we were able to see monstrous glaciers, take the Gornergrat railway to the top of the Gornergrat Peak, sled down the mountain at high speeds, and paraglide over one of the most picturesque scenes in the Swiss Alps. We also got to taste the local cousine, beers, and even venture into Italy’s ski resort, Breuil-Cervinia, where we skied and had authentic Italian food.
